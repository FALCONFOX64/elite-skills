# Framework Crib Sheet — Elite Viral Marketing Manager

The proven models this persona draws on, with when-to-use guidance. These
are lenses, not scripts — if the audience can name the framework, it was
applied too loudly.

---

## Virality & Sharing Psychology

### STEPPS — Jonah Berger, *Contagious*
**What**: six drivers of word-of-mouth — Social currency, Triggers,
Emotion, Public visibility, Practical value, Stories.
**Use for**: the Shareability Check's theoretical backbone; diagnosing why
a good post didn't spread (usually: no social currency for the sharer, or
no trigger tying it to daily life).

### SUCCESs — Heath brothers, *Made to Stick*
**What**: sticky ideas are Simple, Unexpected, Concrete, Credible,
Emotional, Stories.
**Use for**: final pass on any hook or campaign message. The levers most
often missing in marketing content: Concrete (picturable specifics) and
Credible (a real number or named source).

### Hook → Story → Offer — Brunson
**What**: earn attention, transfer belief through narrative, then ask.
**Use for**: campaign arcs and video scripts. Marketing's structural
advantage: real growth war stories with real numbers are inherently better
stories than invented brand narratives.

### AIDA / PAS
**What**: Attention-Interest-Desire-Action; Problem-Agitate-Solve.
**Use for**: conversion-stage copy (landing pages, launch emails, CTAs).
PAS for problem-aware audiences; AIDA for unaware. On technical audiences
compress AIDA to Attention → Evidence → Action — visible Desire stages
read as manipulation there.

## Audience & Positioning

### Jobs-to-be-Done
**What**: audiences "hire" content for a job — learn, feel seen, be
entertained, look smart by sharing.
**Use for**: content pillar design. Each pillar should map to one job the
target audience hires content for. Posts serving no job get scrolled past
regardless of quality.

### 1,000 True Fans — Kevin Kelly
**What**: a small number of deeply invested fans beats a large shallow
audience for any creator or niche business.
**Use for**: re-anchoring users chasing raw follower counts; justifying
the community flywheel and owned-channel investments; the strategic answer
to "when NOT to chase viral."

### Positioning — Dunford, *Obviously Awesome*
**What**: deliberately choose competitive frame, unique value, and who
cares most.
**Use for**: the account-level one-sentence promise (bio, pinned post,
pillar weights) — the first layer of every growth audit.

## Growth Systems

### The Community Flywheel
**What**: reach → regulars → owned home → member stories → better content
→ more reach; each turn cheaper and higher-trust than the last.
**Use for**: the strategic backbone of any 6-month-plus plan. The flywheel
is why the engagement loop is non-negotiable daily work rather than
optional polish.

### COPE — Create Once, Publish Everywhere (adapted)
**What**: one anchor idea, natively re-spoken per platform.
**Use as**: the repurposing waterfall. The adaptation matters: publish
everywhere *natively* — mechanical cross-posting is the failure mode the
original acronym accidentally encourages.

### Retention-First Video Structure
**What**: rank-by-completion feeds (TikTok, Shorts, Reels) make the
retention curve the product. Structure = open loop → escalating beats →
payoff placed after the natural drop point → loopable ending.
**Use for**: every short-form script; long-form YouTube as chained loops.
The discipline popularized by top YouTube practitioners: decide title +
thumbnail first, then build the video that pays them off.

### Funnel-Stage Metric Map
**What**: one metric per growth stage —
| Stage | Optimize | Ignore (for now) |
|---|---|---|
| Reach | impressions per post, hook rate | follower count |
| Growth | follower velocity, profile-visit→follow % | raw impressions |
| Engagement | replies + saves per post, return rate | reach spikes |
| Conversion | owned-channel signups, stage-metric per post | everything else |
**Use for**: Principle 10 enforcement; the audit's metric-goal match; the
weekly review's step 2. Multi-metric optimization is optimization of
nothing.

## Ethics Line (built into every framework above)

Persuasion architecture is applied to *true things offered generously*:
honest hooks that get cashed, emotion that the content genuinely delivers,
scarcity only when real. The moment a framework is used to make a false
thing feel true, it stops being marketing and starts being the reason
audiences install ad blockers. The compounding asset is trust; every
tactic is evaluated against it.

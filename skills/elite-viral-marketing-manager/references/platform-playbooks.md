# Platform Playbooks — Elite Viral Marketing Manager

Per-platform mechanics and tactics. **Honesty header**: ranking systems
change quarterly. Entries marked *(durable)* are structural and stable;
entries marked *(meta, early 2026)* reflect current behavior at knowledge
cutoff and should be re-verified against recent creator data before betting
a campaign on them. When in doubt, run a 2-week test — your account's data
outranks any playbook, including this one.

---

## X / Twitter

**What the feed rewards** *(durable)*: early engagement velocity (first
30-60 min), replies > reposts > likes as signals, dwell time on the post.
*(meta, early 2026)*: heavy weighting toward reply-chains and creator
subscriptions; external links suppress reach — put links in a reply or
follow-up, not the main post.

**Tactics**: hook line must survive truncation; longposts and threads for
depth, single bangers for reach; quote-post with a real take rather than
bare repost; reply to large adjacent accounts with substance (borrowed
reach); pin your best converter.
**Cadence**: 1-3 posts/day + 15-30 min of genuine replies.
**Kills reach**: link-in-post, engagement-bait phrasing ("RT if..."),
posting into a dead timezone.

## LinkedIn

**What the feed rewards** *(durable)*: dwell time (slow scrollers reading
your post), comments with substance, early engagement from your network.
*(meta, early 2026)*: strong preference for native documents/carousels and
text-with-personal-story; outbound links suppressed — same reply-link
workaround as X.

**Tactics**: first 2 lines are the hook (everything else is behind "...see
more"); white space aggressively — one sentence per line reads faster;
personal story wrapped around a professional lesson is the native genre;
comment on your ICP's posts daily (profile visits convert here).
**Cadence**: 3-5/wk. **Kills reach**: corporate-brochure voice, external
links, posting and ghosting.

## TikTok

**What the feed rewards** *(durable)*: completion rate and rewatches above
all; shares and saves next; follower count nearly irrelevant to
distribution — every video auditions to a fresh test audience.
*(meta, early 2026)*: search is a major discovery surface — keyword your
on-screen text, captions, and spoken words (TikTok SEO is real).

**Tactics**: hook in second 1, visual + verbal simultaneously; pattern
interrupts every 3-5s; payoff after the natural drop point; loopable
endings; native text/captions always (sound-off viewers); ride sounds
while they're rising.
**Cadence**: 4-7/wk minimum while learning. **Kills reach**: watermarked
reposts, slow intros, ignoring the first-frame thumbnail.

## Instagram (Reels, Carousels, Stories)

**What the feed rewards** *(durable)*: sends (DM shares) are the strongest
signal, then saves, then comments. Reels for reach, carousels for depth
and saves, stories for the existing audience's loyalty.
*(meta, early 2026)*: original audio and photo-carousels both resurgent;
aggressive suppression of visibly reposted TikToks.

**Tactics**: design carousels for the save ("keep this"); Reels follow the
TikTok script rules; stories = behind-scenes + polls/questions (cheap
engagement signals that boost your feed placement); grid is your landing
page — pin the three best converters.
**Cadence**: 3-4 feed/wk + near-daily stories.

## YouTube (long-form + Shorts)

**The double gate** *(durable)*: CTR on the title+thumbnail gets the click;
retention curve keeps the video recommended. Both or nothing. Title and
thumbnail are 50% of the work — decide them *before* scripting so the
video is built to pay them off.

**Tactics**: open by confirming the click's promise in ≤15s, no channel
intros; structure long-form as chained open loops; Shorts follow TikTok
rules and act as top-of-funnel for the channel; end screens route to the
next-best video, not "any video".
**Cadence**: long 1/wk sustained beats 3/wk then burnout. Search-driven
("how to X") videos compound for years — mix evergreen search targets with
browse-driven topical bets.

## Reddit

**What works** *(durable — Reddit's culture IS its algorithm)*: expertise
generously shared, war stories with real numbers, answering questions in
your domain for weeks before any mention of your product. Every subreddit
has its own law — read the rules and the all-time top posts first.

**Tactics**: post as a practitioner, not a brand; share the lesson and let
people ask what you built; do an honest "we built X, here's what went
wrong" post-mortem (Reddit's favorite genre); AMAs after you've earned
regulars' recognition.
**Hard rule**: promotion ≤ 1-in-10 contributions, and only where rules
allow. **Kills you**: any whiff of astroturfing — Reddit doesn't downrank
marketers, it hunts them.

## Threads

*(meta, early 2026)*: conversational, low-polish, reply-driven; the feed
heavily boosts posts that start conversations; hashtags minimal (single
topic tag). Treat as X's friendlier cousin: same thread mechanics, warmer
voice, less news-jacking. Good second platform for an X-native strategy at
near-zero marginal cost via the waterfall.

## Bluesky

*(meta, early 2026)*: chronological-first with custom feeds; community
skews technical/early-adopter; discovery via starter packs and custom
feeds rather than an engagement algorithm. What works: being genuinely
present and conversational; niche custom feeds are underpriced
distribution. Low volume, high trust — treat it as community, not reach.

## Mastodon / Fediverse

*(durable by design)*: no engagement algorithm at all — chronological,
boost-driven. Hashtags actually matter for discovery. Culture is strongly
anti-marketing: participate as a person, use CWs per instance norms, and
let boosts carry reach. Only worth staffing if your audience genuinely
lives there (OSS, infosec, academia).

## Facebook

**What still works** *(meta, early 2026)*: Groups (own one in your niche —
it's an owned community channel with organic reach), Reels sharing the
Instagram pipeline, local/event and 35+ demographics. Page organic reach
remains near-dead; don't build a strategy on it. Groups are the reason
Facebook stays on this list.
